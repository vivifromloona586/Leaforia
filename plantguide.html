Home Planting Guide

Below, you can explore simple and easy guides on how to plant properly, choose suitable locations for the plants, and care for them effectively. You can also learn about common mistakes people often make while planting and how to avoid them to help your plants grow healthier and stronger.

What You Need

  • Pot with drainage holes
  • Potting soil
  • Small home plant or seeds
  • Water spray bottle
  • Small stones

Planting Steps

  1. Put small stones at bottom of pot for better drainage.
  2. Fill the pot halfway with potting soil
  3. Carefully place the plant in the center of the pot
  4. Gently cover the roots with remaning soil and press the soil lightly so the plant stays stable.
  5. Water gently using a spray bottle until the soil becomes slightly moist.
  6. If indoors, place the plant near a window that could provide appropriate sunlight

Choosing The Right Spot

  • Place plants near a window with natural sunlight.
  • Avoid putting plants under strong afternoon heat because leaves may dry out.
  • Keep plants in a place with fresh air circulation.
  • Keep plants away from air conditioners or fans that can dry the soil quickly.
  • Choose a safe place where pets or small children will not damage the plant.

Plant Care Tips

  • Water plants regularly but avoid making the soil too wet.
  • Remove dry or yellow leaves to keep the plant healthy.
  • Rotate and turn pots every 1–2 weeks so all sides receive sunlight evenly.
  • Check for insects or pests on the leaves and stems and try to eliminate them if possible.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Overwatering plants can cause root rot and damage the plant.
  • Using pots without drainage holes can trap too much water.
  • Placing plants in very dark areas may slow their growth.
  • Forgetting to water plants regularly can dry the soil completely.
  • Adding too much fertilizer may harm the roots.
  • Moving plants too often can stress the plant and affect growth.

ROOFTOP GARDENING

If you have an unused rooftop in your home, you can turn it into a refreshing green corner where fresh ingredients can grow and cooler surroundings and cleaner air naturally appear. With enough sunlight, proper watering and simple planting spaces, even busy urban areas can feel more alive and sustainable. Learn some methods you can easily follow at home below

CONTAINER GARDENING

This method involves growing plants in containers such as pots, planters or raised beds. It provides a versatile and accessible way to create a flourishing garden in a limited space.

Good for:Tomatoes, peppers, leafy greens, herbs and flowers.

VERTICAL GARDENING

Vertical gardening is an innovative and space-saving method that allows you to utilize vertical spaces on your home terrace or roof. It involves growing plants vertically using walls, trellises or specialized structures.

Good for:Climbing plants, vines, and small vegetables.

HYDROPONICS

Hydroponics is a soilless gardening method that involves growing plants in water with added nutrients. It can be effectively used on rooftops, providing an efficient and space-saving way to cultivate many plants.

Good for:Lettuce, herbs, spinach, and strawberries.
