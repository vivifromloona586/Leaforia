Easy Plants You
Can Grow At Home

The plants featured below are beginner-friendly selections which are easy to maintain, and suitable for Myanmar’s warm climate with perfect appearnces for homes, balconies and, indoor spaces

Snake Plant

The snake plant, or Sansevieria trifasciata, is a tropical plant that is incredibly resilient and can tolerate a wide range of lighting conditions, from low light to bright indirect sunlight, and only needs watering about once every two to three weeks though it may need a little more frequent watering during the brighter spring and summer months and less frequent during the darker fall and winter seasons. If you’re wary of overwatering, you can wait for its usually stiff leaves to start to wrinkle, which is a sign of thirst. The snake plant has a modified form of photosynthesis in which it opens its pores only at night to preserve water, storing the carbon dioxide it absorbs for use during the daytime. Because of this process, it also releases oxygen at night, helping improve indoor air quality.

Monstera

The Monstera Deliciosa is considered an easy-care plant, requiring very little attention to grow full and lush.With the right care, Monsteras are very fast growing plants. They only need bright indirect light to grow fast. On average, Monsteras will grow around 2 feet per year. Be sure to regularly fertilize your Monstera for faster growth! In the wild, monsteras use their aerial roots to attach to trees or branches nearby, growing upward towards the light. If you want to grow a very large monstera over time, you will need to mimic its natural habitat by providing it a stake or moss pole to grow vertically. However, Monsteras are toxic to cats and dogs. While mild ingestion rarely causes major harm, it is best to avoid this plant entirely if you have pets.

Peace Lily

The Peace Lily, known for its elegant white flowers and glossy green leaves, is also a great choice as it can grow well in warm and humid conditions with low to medium indirect light while direct sunlight can burn the leaves. The plant only needs watering when the soil becomes dry, so it is easy to maintain for beginners and busy people. They can also help improve indoor air quality and adapt well to indoor environments with proper humidity and warmth.

Pothos

Pothos are popular indoor plants with heart-shaped leaves and long trailing vines. They grow successfully in small pots, hanging baskets, and water containers. Pothos can survive well in low light and warm temperatures, making them suitable for homes, classrooms, and offices. Myanmar’s tropical climate supports healthy growth with fresh green leaves throughout the year. Their attractive appearance and low maintenance make pothos a very commonly grown decorative plants indoors.

Aglaonema

Aglaonema plants, also known as Chinese evergreens, are some of the most popular houseplants around since they're easy to care for, and come in a variety of attractive colours and patterns. Aglaonema plants prefer bright, indirect light, such as that found near a north or east-facing window. Avoid direct sunlight, as this can scorch the leaves. If your aglaonema is not getting enough light, its leaves may turn pale or yellow. Aglaonemas prefer warm temperatures, between 18 and 26°C. Try to avoid exposing them to cold draughts or extreme temperatures. Aglaonemas should be watered when the soil is dry to the touch. When watering, make sure to water thoroughly until water runs out of the drainage holes. Allow the top inch or two of soil to dry out before watering again. Overwatering can cause root rot, so it’s better to underwater than to overwater.

Spider Plant

Spider plants are admired for their elegant striped leaves and graceful hanging stems that add a fresh and peaceful feeling to indoor spaces. Their lively green appearance remains vibrant in warm and humid conditions, allowing the plant to grow steadily throughout the year. Small offshoots that grow from the main plant create a unique cascading effect, making spider plants especially attractive in hanging baskets and decorative pots. Their neat appearance and natural beauty make them a popular choice for creating a relaxing and refreshing atmosphere indoors.
