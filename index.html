Introduction


Welcome to our website, where you can explore the importance of plants and learn how small planting efforts can contribute to a greener and more sustainable environment. Here, you can learn about the role plants play in improving air quality by producing oxygen and absorbing harmful gases from the atmosphere, which clears the air and make it more healthy. Our suggestions for plants that are suitable for our country, Myanmar's climate and a guide of how to care for them properly are also available, so that whenever you feel like planting, you can easily look through our website and get started!

Our Objective

Our aim is to encourage individuals to actively contribute to the growth of green spaces within our community by planting more—it doesn’t have to be many, even one plant for each home is enough to make a significant difference if we all united to plant. We also aim to promote the positive impact plants have on the environment. By raising awareness, we hope to inspire everyone to start small and take part in building a cooler, healthier, and more sustainable environment for all.

Enviromental Benefits Of Planting

Cleaner Air

Plants act as natural air filters, absorbing carbon dioxide and pollutants while releasing fresh oxygen.

Cooler Cities

Greenery reduces urban heat by providing shade and releasing water vapour into the air.

Healthier Living

Being around plants improves well-being and creates a more relaxing environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use organic fertilizers or synthetic fertilizers for my plants?

Organic fertilizers are environmentally friendly, improve long-term soil health and cause less water pollution but they can be slower to act as the nutrients must be slowly broken down by soil microbes before plants can absorb them. While synthetic fertilizers offer instant plant growth with precise nutrients, they can burn the plants if overapplied and the material from the manufacture have a negative impact on the environment.
• If you don't need an instant growth and want to promote sustainability, choose organic.
• If you're in a rush and need immediate growth, choose synthetic.

What kind of organic fertilizers should I use for my plants?

There is a variety of natural options you can choose from:
• Banana peels: A fantastic source of potassium.
• Coffee grounds: Packed with nitrogen; ideal for plants that need a little acidity in their soil
• Eggshells: Contain essential nutrients like calcium and phosphorus.
• Epsom salts: A great source of minerals like magnesium.
• Green tea: Leaves are packed with nutrients, though only suitable for plants that prefer slightly acidic soil.

How deep do I put the seeds?

As a general rule, plant seeds two to three times as deep as their diameter or width. Smaller seeds need to be very close to the surface, while larger seeds require more soil on top to anchor the roots and provide energy for the shoot to push through.

How do I keep pests away?

Ensure every pot has several drainage holes and excess is not left in the bottom of your pots. Soil that stays wet for too long creates the perfect environment for pests like fungus gnats. Clean plants by occasionally wiping down the leaves with a damp cloth to remove dust and potential pests. Avoid Crowding to ensure adequate space between your plants to improve air circulation and reduce the risk of pest spread.

How much sunlight do indoor and balcony plants need to grow properly?

Indoor and balcony plants generally need 6 to 8 hours of light daily, but exact needs vary by plant type.While some plants can tolerate low-light conditions, most indoor plants grow healthier and look better with consistent, adequate light exposure.
